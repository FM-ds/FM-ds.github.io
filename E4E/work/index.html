Negative Income Taxes

Negative income Taxes provide cash to residents earning under a certain threshold. A worker earning under the threshold would be given cash proportional to the difference between their income and a cutoff, guaranteeing a minimum income while not disincentivizing work.

The Four Experiments

The idea became popular in the 1960s with the 1962 publication of Milton Friedman's "Capitalism and Freedom". Between 1968 and 1982, the United States sponsored four negative income tax experiments.

The Results

The results of each study suggest a small negative effect on employment rates, and on quantity of work.
